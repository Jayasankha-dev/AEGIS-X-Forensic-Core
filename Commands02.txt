This guide explains how to identify and terminate unauthorized background services (Service Workers) that may be leaking data or tracking your activity 
without your consent.

1. The Entry Point: Notifications
URL: chrome://settings/content/notifications

Purpose: Malicious sites often trick users into clicking "Allow" on a notification pop-up. This is the "hook" they use to install a Service Worker.

What to do: Look at the "Allowed to send notifications" list. If you see sites you don't recognize (especially shopping, betting, or random string sites), 
remove them immediately.

2. The Engine: Service Worker Internals
URL: chrome://serviceworker-internals/

Purpose: This page lists every background script currently registered in your browser. Service Workers can run even if the website's tab is closed.

What to check: * Scope: Look for URLs that don't belong to trusted services like Google, YouTube, or your installed extensions.

Registration Count: A typical user has 5-15. If you see 50+, your browser is likely being used for data harvesting or ad-fraud.

The Fix: Use the "Unregister" button for suspicious entries, or use the Console script to wipe them all at once.

3. The Evidence: Network Export
URL: chrome://net-export/

Purpose: This is a forensic tool that captures every single packet of data entering or leaving the browser.

How to use: 1. Click "Start Logging to Disk".
2. Browse for a few minutes.
3. Stop logging and upload the .json file to a tool like NetLog Viewer.

What we look for: We search for "Established" connections to foreign IP addresses (like the Alibaba/Taobao IPs we found) 
that occur while you aren't even visiting those sites.
